Indian Nationals: Physical Stamps are No Longer Needed!

Under the updated 2026 guidelines, Indian nationals do not need to stand in lines at the Leh DC office. Simply fill out your itinerary details at lahdclehpermit.in, pay the EDF online, and download the PDF. Print at least 10 copies to distribute at checkposts like South Pullu, North Pullu, Loma, and Mahe Bridge.

CRITICAL: Non-Local Rental Bike Sightseeing Ban

The Leh Ladakh Bike Union strictly prohibits motorcycles rented outside the Ladakh region (e.g. from Delhi, Srinagar, or Manali) from being used for sightseeing in Nubra Valley, Pangong Tso, and Hanle. If riding a rental bike from Delhi: You must park it in Leh and rent a local Leh-registered bike for the Nubra-Pangong-Hanle-Tso Moriri sightseeing circuit. If riding your own personal bike: Ensure the Registration Certificate (RC) is original and in your name, or carry a notarized NOC from the owner with matching IDs.

AMS Severity Common Symptoms Immediate Response Action Plan
Mild AMS (Common) Mild headache, mild fatigue, slight dizziness, loss of appetite, disturbed sleep. Rest in place, avoid physical exertion, hydrate with ORS/Water. Take Diamox (250mg) if headache persists. Do not ascend further.
Severe AMS (Dangerous) Persistent vomiting, severe chest tightness, irregular breathing, dry hacking cough, extreme fatigue. Administer portable oxygen cylinder. Start immediate, gradual descent of 300-500 meters. Prepare evacuation to Leh hospital.
Life Threatening (HACE / HAPE) Confusion, slurred speech, inability to walk in a straight line, blue lips, coughing blood. EMERGENCY EVACUATION. Descend immediately to below 3,000m. Administer continuous oxygen. Rush to nearest SNM Hospital in Leh.
Acclimatization Rules
  • MANDATORY 48 hours rest in Leh upon arrival (Day 4 & 5).
  • Drink 3 litres of water daily. Add ORS/electrolytes.
  • Absolutely NO alcohol or sleeping pills during the first 48 hours in Leh.
  • Eat light, carb-dense meals; heavy proteins worsen altitude sickness.
Prophylaxis (Diamox Guide)
  • Medication: Diamox 250mg (Acetazolamide).
  • Dosage: Half tab (125mg) or 1 tab (250mg) twice daily after meals.
  • Timing: Start on June 6 (2 days BEFORE arriving in Leh) and continue until descending Manali side.
  • Note: Diamox is a diuretic; you will need to urinate frequently. Stay hydrated.

Digital & UPI Payment Warning

Do not rely on digital payments or mobile wallets. Outside Leh, Srinagar, and Manali, most remote spots have no mobile internet or cellular network for processing online transactions. ATMs are either empty or non-existent in Hanle, Tso Moriri, and Sarchu.
